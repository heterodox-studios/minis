# VÄGGRULLE Technical documentation

A wall-mounted paper roll holder. Paper feeds from the bottom, up the wall, and rolls onto a take-up roll at the top. Magnets hold the paper in place so it can be written on like a whiteboard. Two integrated shelves provide space for writing accessories.

## Safety and Risk Assessment

This section records the manufacturer's internal safety assessment performed under Article 9 of Regulation (EU) 2023/988 (GPSR). It is reviewed when the design, suppliers, materials or intended use change, and at minimum annually.

### Intended users and intended use

The product is intended for adults, for use in homes and offices as a wall-mounted writing surface and shelf. Installation requires basic DIY competence (drill, screwdriver, spirit level) and is intended to be performed by an adult.

### Foreseeable use, including misuse

Per GPSR Article 7, the assessment considers reasonably foreseeable use — including by categories of consumer at particular risk (in particular children) — even where the product is not designed for them. The following foreseeable scenarios have been considered:

- Children present in the home or office where the unit is mounted.
- A child reaching for the loose magnets, the upper shelf, or items placed on the upper shelf (e.g. permanent markers).
- A child or pet pulling on the paper roll.
- Adult misuse of the lower shelf as a step or hand-hold.
- Overloading of either shelf.
- Mounting on an unsuitable wall, with unsuitable fixings, or at an unsuitable height.
- Placement near sources of ignition (candles, incense, tea-lights, hobs).
- Use by adults with pacemakers, implanted medical devices, or in proximity to magnetic storage media.

### Hazards considered, and mitigations

| Hazard | Risk source | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unit detaching from wall | Inadequate fixings, overloaded shelves, wall material incompatible with supplied screws | Assembly instructions specify wall types the supplied screws are appropriate for; instructions direct the user to source alternative fixings for masonry or unbacked plasterboard; instructions specify "do not overload"; product is light (~2 kg empty) which limits potential injury. |
| Magnet ingestion (small children) | Magnets are user-removable during normal use (placed on the writing surface, removed to advance paper); two magnets ingested separately can attract through bowel tissue with serious consequences | Product not designed or marketed for children; warning and instructions direct users to keep magnets out of reach of small children; magnets are powder-coated (not bare neodymium). **Residual risk acknowledged: see "Residual risks" below.** |
| Magnet interaction with implanted medical devices | Neodymium magnets can affect pacemakers and ICDs at close range | Warning in user-facing safety information and on the product label. |
| Magnet damage to magnetic storage media | Strong magnetic field near disks, magnetic-stripe cards | Warning in user-facing safety information. |
| Fire | Paper is flammable, mounted close to wall | Warning against placing sources of ignition on or near the unit. |
| Sharp edges, pinch points during installation | Folded steel parts, screws | Edges deburred during fabrication; all corners are rounded; assembly is low-force. |
| Chemical hazards | Powder coating, plastic spacers, magnet coating | REACH compliance confirmed by suppliers (no SVHCs > 0.1% w/w); no painted or coated surfaces in contact with food. |
| Falling objects from upper shelf | Permanent markers or similar items on small upper shelf | Shelf depth limits load; lip prevents rolling off; warning against overloading; shelf height places it above typical small-child reach when correctly mounted. |

### Residual risks

The dominant residual risk is **magnet ingestion by small children**. The mitigations (not marketed for children, warnings) reduce but do not eliminate this risk. The risk is judged acceptable on the basis that:

- The magnets are the same class of object adults routinely use unsupervised (fridge magnets, magnetic note holders) and are not shaped to resemble food.
- Equivalent risk exists in many ordinary household items not subject to specific restriction.
- Clear warnings are provided in the assembly instructions and the technical documentation.

The next most significant residual risk is **wall-mount failure**, mitigated by fixings appropriate to the most common wall types and by clear instructions to source alternative fixings where required. Residual risk is judged acceptable.

### Conclusion

The product, used for its intended purpose by an adult and installed per the supplied instructions, presents no significant residual risk to health or safety. Foreseeable misuse risks have been identified and mitigated through design, materials selection, instructions, and on-product warnings. The product is assessed as compliant with the general safety requirement in Article 6 of Regulation (EU) 2023/988.

### Regulatory scope

## User Instructions

### Assembly

The unit is wall-mounted and must be securely fixed to a wall capable of bearing its weight; mounting hardware should be appropriate to the wall material.

Basic installation involves fixing the parts to the wall — see the separate [assembly document](./assembly-instructions.md).

### Maintenance and cleaning advice

No maintenance required.

Wipe clean with a soft, damp cloth. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals.


## Safety Information

The customer-facing safety summary is provided in [safety.md](./safety.md). The key points are reproduced here for completeness:

Ensure the unit is fixed to a wall capable of supporting its weight, using fixings appropriate to the wall material.

Do not overload the shelves.

Paper is flammable — do not place sources of ignition (e.g. lit candles, incense, tea-lights) on the shelves or near the unit.

Keep the magnets out of reach of small children, and away from pacemakers and other implanted medical devices, and from magnetic storage media.
